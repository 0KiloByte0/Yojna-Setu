Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) is a flagship scheme launched by the Government of India on 8th April 2015 to provide financial support to small and micro-enterprises. The scheme is implemented through Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency (MUDRA) Ltd., a subsidiary of SIDBI (Small Industries Development Bank of India).


🔍 Objective:

To provide collateral-free loans to small/micro business enterprises, encouraging self-employment and entrepreneurship, especially among youth, women, and marginalized communities.


💰 Loan Categories under PMMY:

PMMY offers three categories of loans based on the stage of business development:

  1. Shishu – Loans up to ₹50,000
    (For startups or early-stage businesses)
  2. Kishore – Loans from ₹50,001 to ₹5 lakh
    (For businesses in the growth phase)
  3. Tarun – Loans from ₹5 lakh to ₹10 lakh
    (For well-established businesses requiring further expansion)


🏦 Eligible Lending Institutions:

  • Public Sector Banks
  • Private Sector Banks
  • Regional Rural Banks (RRBs)
  • Small Finance Banks
  • Micro Finance Institutions (MFIs)
  • Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs)


👥 Who Can Apply:

  • Small manufacturing units
  • Shopkeepers and traders
  • Artisans and craftsmen
  • Service sector businesses
  • Individuals planning to start small enterprises


📋 Features:

  • No collateral required
  • Reasonable interest rates (varies by lender)
  • Flexible repayment terms (up to 5 years)
  • Encourages formalization of the informal sector


📈 Impact:

Since its launch, PMMY has sanctioned over ₹22 lakh crore to more than 40 crore beneficiaries (as of 2024), with a strong focus on:

  • Women entrepreneurs (around 68% of loans)
  • SC/ST/OBC borrowers
  • First-time business owners
